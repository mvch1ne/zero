# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

SprintLab exists to give every athlete access to the kind of biomechanics analysis that was previously only available in well-funded labs. That mission only works if everyone who contributes, reports issues, or asks questions feels welcome and respected — regardless of age, body type, athletic background, disability, ethnicity, gender identity, experience level, nationality, personal appearance, religion, or sexual identity.

We pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone.

## Our Standards

**Behaviour that contributes to a positive environment:**

- Being kind and patient, especially with people who are new to the project or to software development
- Giving and receiving constructive feedback graciously
- Acknowledging that people come from different backgrounds — a sprinting coach and a software engineer may have very different mental models, and both are valuable
- Focusing criticism on ideas and code, not on people
- Taking responsibility when you make a mistake, apologising, and moving on

**Behaviour that is not acceptable:**

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Dismissing or belittling someone's question because it seems obvious to you
- Publishing someone's private information without explicit permission
- Personal attacks or sustained disruptive behaviour in issues, pull requests, or discussions

## Enforcement

If you witness or experience unacceptable behaviour, please open a private issue or email the maintainer directly. All reports will be reviewed and investigated. The maintainer reserves the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, and other contributions that violate this code of conduct, and to temporarily or permanently ban contributors whose behaviour is harmful.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other venue where you are representing the project.
